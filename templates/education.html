Brain Tumor Detection

Advanced segmentation model for detecting and analyzing brain tumors from MRI scans

0.73 Dice Score
U-Net Architecture
MRI Modality

Overview

Our brain tumor detection model utilizes advanced U-Net architecture to perform precise segmentation of brain tumors from MRI scans. The model can identify various types of brain tumors including gliomas, meningiomas, and pituitary tumors.

Multi-class tumor segmentation
3D volume analysis
Tumor size quantification

Technical Architecture

Model Type: U-Net with ResNet backbone
Input Size: 256x256x3 (RGB)
Output: Segmentation mask
Training Data: 2,500+ annotated MRI scans
Validation: 5-fold cross-validation

Clinical Applications

Diagnostic Support

Assist radiologists in identifying and localizing brain tumors during initial screening

Treatment Planning

Provide precise tumor measurements for surgical planning and radiation therapy

Monitoring

Track tumor progression and treatment response over time

Limitations & Considerations

  • Requires high-quality MRI scans with proper contrast
  • May struggle with very small tumors (< 5mm)
  • Performance varies with tumor type and location
  • Should be used as a decision support tool, not replacement for expert diagnosis

Chest X-ray Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of chest X-rays for detecting pulmonary abnormalities and diseases

95% Accuracy
ResNet-50 Architecture
X-ray Modality

Overview

Our chest X-ray analysis model uses deep learning to detect various pulmonary conditions including pneumonia, COVID-19, tuberculosis, and other respiratory diseases. The model provides both classification and localization of abnormalities.

Multi-disease classification
Abnormality localization
Confidence scoring

Technical Architecture

Model Type: ResNet-50 with attention mechanism
Input Size: 224x224x1 (Grayscale)
Output: Multi-class probabilities + heatmap
Training Data: 50,000+ chest X-rays
Classes: Normal, Pneumonia, COVID-19, Tuberculosis

Clinical Applications

Emergency Screening

Rapid screening for COVID-19 and pneumonia in emergency departments

Routine Check-ups

Automated analysis of routine chest X-rays for early disease detection

Resource Optimization

Prioritize cases requiring immediate attention in high-volume settings

Limitations & Considerations

  • Requires proper X-ray positioning and exposure
  • May miss subtle findings in early disease stages
  • Performance affected by patient positioning and image quality
  • Should be validated with clinical correlation

Skin Cancer Detection

Advanced classification model for detecting malignant skin lesions from dermoscopic images

93% Accuracy
EfficientNet Architecture
Dermoscopy Modality

Overview

Our skin cancer detection model analyzes dermoscopic images to classify skin lesions as benign or malignant. The model can detect various types of skin cancer including melanoma, basal cell carcinoma, and squamous cell carcinoma.

Binary classification (Benign/Malignant)
Lesion boundary detection
Risk stratification

Technical Architecture

Model Type: EfficientNet-B4 with custom head
Input Size: 380x380x3 (RGB)
Output: Binary classification + confidence
Training Data: 15,000+ dermoscopic images
Augmentation: Rotation, flip, color jittering

Clinical Applications

Primary Care Screening

Enable general practitioners to screen suspicious skin lesions

Dermatology Triage

Prioritize high-risk cases for immediate dermatologist review

Telemedicine

Support remote skin cancer screening in underserved areas

Limitations & Considerations

  • Requires high-quality dermoscopic images
  • May struggle with atypical presentations
  • Performance varies with skin type and lesion location
  • Should be followed by biopsy for definitive diagnosis

Fracture Detection

Object detection model for identifying and localizing bone fractures in X-ray images

0.94 mAP50
YOLO v8 Architecture
X-ray Modality

Overview

Our fracture detection model uses YOLO (You Only Look Once) architecture to detect and localize bone fractures in X-ray images. The model can identify various types of fractures including stress fractures, complete fractures, and hairline fractures.

Real-time fracture detection
Multiple fracture types
Bounding box localization

Technical Architecture

Model Type: YOLO v8 with CSP backbone
Input Size: 640x640x1 (Grayscale)
Output: Bounding boxes + confidence scores
Training Data: 8,000+ annotated X-rays
Inference Speed: ~30ms per image

Clinical Applications

Emergency Radiology

Rapid fracture detection in emergency departments

Sports Medicine

Quick assessment of sports-related injuries

Orthopedic Screening

Automated screening for fracture patterns

Limitations & Considerations

  • May miss subtle or hairline fractures
  • Performance affected by image quality and positioning
  • Requires proper X-ray technique and exposure
  • Should be correlated with clinical findings
